Insights: The insights from this visualization are that there are certain counties with more unemployment and income change across the years. Geographically speaking in general, counties in northern california and along the coast of California have lower unemployment and greater increases in income. In the time series plots generally speaking, we can observe that unemployment tends to be decreasing as the years go by, and income seems to be increasing although in a more volatile fashion. By rank we also tend to see several repeated counties in the two extremes of the radial bar chart, and more specifically we could see that Imperial County is by far the one with the greatest unemployment. Marin, San Mateo, San Francisco , and Santa Clara are the counties with least unemployment, while Imperial and Colusa tend to be the ones with greatest unemployment. With Respect to income data San Francisco and San Mateo are the ones that in general experienced the greatest increase in income.
